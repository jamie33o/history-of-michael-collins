Collins Facts

Before Collins took up his role as an Irish soldier, Minister, strategist, and revolutionary, Collins lived and worked in London from 1906 as a clerk in a post office. Here, he gained valuable administrative skills that would benefit him throughout the War of Independence

1890: Michael Collins is Born

Michael Collins was born on October 16, 1890, in Woodfield, County Cork, Ireland. He was the youngest of eight children in a farming family. Collins grew up in a rural setting, where he developed a deep love for the Irish countryside and a strong sense of national identity. His father, Michael John Collins, passed away when he was just six years old, leaving his mother, Mary Anne O'Brien Collins, to raise the children on her own. Despite the financial challenges they faced, Collins's mother prioritized education and ensured that he received a good education. Collins attended the local national school and later went on to study at the prestigious Clonakilty National School and the prestigious Castleknock College in Dublin. These formative years in rural Ireland shaped Collins's early life and instilled in him a passion for his country and its struggle for independence
